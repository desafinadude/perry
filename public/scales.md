# Scales & Chords Reference

## Notation Legend

| Symbol | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| `∆` | Major 7th; can mean a major scale (C∆) or a major 7th note (C−∆) |
| `7` | Lowered 7th |
| `–` | Minor (usually Dorian Minor) |
| `°` | Diminished scale or chord |
| `Ø` | Half-diminished scale or chord |
| `+` or `♯` | Raise the note 1/2 step |
| `−` or `♭` | Lower the note 1/2 step |
| `−3` | Minor third interval (= 3 half-steps) |
| `♭9` after a dom. 7th chord | 3 altered tones: ♭9, ♯9 & ♯4 |
| `♯9` after a dom. 7th chord | 4 altered tones: ♭9, ♯9, ♯4 & ♯5 |

> Half-diminished scales are the same as a major scale 1/2 step above.  
> `♯2 = "Half-dim.♯2"`, e.g. BØ♯2 = B C♯ D E F G A B

## Ways to Practice Scales, Melodies, Chords, Intervals & Patterns

The Chromatic Scale is our Musical Alphabet. Start with something simple like "Mary Had A Little Lamb" and play it through all 12 major keys.

1. **Chromatically Up (Ascending):** C C♯ D D♯ E F F♯ G G♯ A B♭ B C
2. **Chromatically Down (Descending):** C B B♭ A A♭ G G♭ F E E♭ D D♭ C
3. **Whole Step Progression – Up:** C D E F♯ G♯ B♭ C / C♯ D♯ F G A B C♯
4. **Whole Step Progression – Down:** C B♭ A♭ G♭ E D C / C♯ B A G F E♭ C♯
5. **Minor Thirds (3 half-steps):** C E♭ G♭ A C / C♯ E G B♭ C♯ / D F A♭ B D
6. **Whole and Half Step Progression:** C D D♭ E♭ D E E♭ F E F♯ F G G♭ A♭ G A A♭ B♭ A B B♭ C B C♯ C
7. **Use tones of any chord or scale:** e.g. C7 Chord: C E G B♭; F♯ Dim. Chord: F♯ A C E♭; Ab7+4 Scale: A♭ B♭ C D E♭ F G♭ A♭
8. **Make up your own sequences.** The ability to play patterns is ~50% intellect and 50% ear at first; the more you play, the higher the ear percentage becomes.

## Introduction to the Scale Syllabus

Each chord/scale symbol (C7, C—, C∆+4, etc.) represents a series of tones for improvising or soloing. The Scale Syllabus is presented in the key of C Concert for easy comparison.

The five most predominant chord/scale families:
- Major
- Dominant 7th
- Dorian Minor
- Blues
- Half-Diminished / Diminished / Suspended 4

Scales near the **top** of each category = mild / consonant.  
Scales further **down** = increasingly tense / dissonant.

> Music is made of tension and release. The improvisor's ability to control tension and release will largely determine their success in communicating to the listener.

## Nomenclature

Because jazz players, composers, educators and authors haven't agreed on a common nomenclature, the novice should become familiar with several different ways of writing the same scale sound.

| Chord/Scale Type | Primary Symbol | Alternate Symbols |
|---|---|---|
| Major (Ionian) WWHWWWH | C∆ | Cmaj, Cma, Cma7, Cmaj7, CM, CM7, Cmaj9, Cmaj13 |
| Dominant Seventh (Mixolydian) WWHWWHW | C7 | C9, C11, C13 |
| Minor Seventh (Dorian) WHWWWHW | C— | C—7, Cmi, Cmi7, Cm7, Cmin, Cmin7, Cm9, Cm11, Cm13 |
| Lydian (Major + ♯4) WWWHWWH | C∆+4 | Cmaj+4, CM+4, C∆+11, C∆♭5, Cmaj♭5 |
| Half-Diminished (Locrian) HWWHWWW | CØ | Cmi7(♭5), C—7♭5 |
| Half-Diminished ♯2 (Locrian ♯2) WHWHWWW | CØ♯2 | CØ+2, CØ9 |
| Diminished WHWHWHWH | C° | Cdim, C°7, Cdim7, C°9 |
| Lydian Dominant (Dom. 7th + ♯4) WWWHWHW | C7+4 | C7+11, C7♭5, C9+11, C13+11 |
| Whole-Tone WWWWWWW | C7+ | C7aug, C7+5 |
| Dominant 7th (Dim. Scale) HWHWHWHW | C7♭9 | C7♭9+4, C13♭9+11 |
| Diminished Whole-Tone (Altered) HWHWWWW | C7+9 | C7alt, C7♭9+4, C7♭9+11 |
| Lydian Augmented (Major + ♯4 & ♯5) WWWWHWH | C∆+4 | C∆+5 |
| Melodic Minor (Ascending) WHWWWWH | C—∆ | Cmin(maj7), Cmi∆, Cm6 |
| Harmonic Minor WHWWH−3H | C—∆ | Cmi∆, C—∆(Har), C—∆♭6 |
| Suspended 4th W−3WWHW or WWHWWHW | G—/C | G—7, C7sus4, C7sus, C4, C11 |
| Blues Scale −3WHH−3W (1, ♭3, 4, ♯4, 5, ♭7, 1) | (none) | Used with dominant and minor chords |

> Note: 2nds = 9ths, 4ths = 11ths, 13ths = 6ths. (e.g. in key of C, the 2nd D = the 9th D)
